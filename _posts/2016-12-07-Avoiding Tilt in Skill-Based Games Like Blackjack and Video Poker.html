Avoiding Tilt in Skill-Based Games Like Blackjack and Video Poke

By: jeddleman, Friday September 6th 2013
0 Comments   Email   Print

Tilt is a term in the online gambling world that describes a scenario where you aren't playing your best because of factors like fatigue, frustration or distractions. There are many degrees of tilt, but there's one thing for certain: The more you work to avoid tilt, the better your end results will be. Tilt mostly affects players who enjoy skill-based games like blackjack and video poker because those are the games where not being on the top of your game will most drastically change your bottom line. Here we're going to offer some pointers on how to avoid this condition and maximize your earnings.

External factors contribute to tilt, and they are the easiest things to avoid when you're playing. A major part of avoiding this type of thing is to create a situation where you are comfortable and focused by cultivating a good environment for your play. This includes turning off instant messanger programs, getting snacks and a drink before you start playing and turning off the television.

Internal factors also play a major role in how you play. For example, if you're tired from a long day of work, then it might not be the best time to play a skill-based game unless you feel like you can really focus on the task at hand. If you want a game to play without having to focus too much, then try Baccarat. The main two bets in Baccarat offer payout rates of close to 99 percent which is similar to what skilled amateurs will achieve in video poker and blackjack on the Internet. This is a good alternative because you can't really tilt in the game of Baccarat unless you start choosing bad bets like the tie bet.

Winning and losing streaks can also cause you to tilt. If you win a lot in a short period of time, then you start to let your guard down a little and focus less, and this will make you play worse overall. On the other hand, losing a lot in a short period of time can make you aggravated and angry, and this can lead to things like betting more than your bankroll can handle and making bad betting decisions. If you have a long streak, consider just quitting for a while instead.

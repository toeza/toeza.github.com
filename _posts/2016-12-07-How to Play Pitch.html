How to Play Pitch

Community Q&A

This game is rumored to have been invented by a bored hunting party in the 1800s. Pitch is a game that revolves around betting and trick-taking to try to score the most points. Learn the basic guidelines of Pitch by reading on.

Steps

  1. 1 Pitch requires at least 3 people. The dealer shuffles and then deals out six cards to each player, in the pattern of 1, then 2, then 3 cards per deal. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/99/Play-Pitch-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/9/99/Play-Pitch-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-1-preview-Version-2.jpg /4/43/Play Pitch Step 1 Version 2.360p.mp4
  2. 2 Now everyone has to bid. How much you bid corresponds to how much confidence you have in the hand you've been dealt. The bidding starts with the person to the left of the dealer, and goes in a circle. You can bid 2, 3, or 4 cards. The bid represents the number of points that you think you will get in this hand. If you aren't confident at all, you may pass, unless you are the last to bid (i.e. the dealer) and everyone else has passed, in which case you have to bid at least 2. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/2/2d/Play-Pitch-Step-2-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-2-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/2/2d/Play-Pitch-Step-2-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-2-preview-Version-2.jpg /e/e0/Play Pitch Step 2 Version 2.360p.mp4
  3. 3 Once someone has placed a bid, you have to match them, surpass them, or pass on your turn. That is, you cannot bid 2 if 3 has already been bid. The point of bidding is to figure out who is the most confident in his or her hand, because that is the person who is going to get to choose the trump suit. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/1/18/Play-Pitch-Step-3-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-3-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/1/18/Play-Pitch-Step-3-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-3-preview-Version-2.jpg /d/db/Play Pitch Step 3 Version 2.360p.mp4
  4. 4 What to bid depends on what your hand is, in relation to the points of pitch. The points that you can get in pitch are these: high of trump, low of trump, jack of trump, and game. "High" is the highest trump card played, "low" is the lowest trump card played, "jack" is the jack of trump, and "game" has to do with how many face cards you end up with. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/4/42/Play-Pitch-Step-4-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-4-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/4/42/Play-Pitch-Step-4-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-4-preview-Version-2.jpg /7/78/Play Pitch Step 4 Version 2.360p.mp4
  5. 5 Which suit becomes trump depends on who bids the highest, because whoever bids the highest gets to throw down first, and the first card that they throw down becomes the trump suit. Therefore, if you have the ace of spades, the 2 of spades, and the jack of spades, you probably want to bid 4, because then you will get to make "spades" the trump suit and you will probably be able to get 4 points. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/0/0b/Play-Pitch-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/0/0b/Play-Pitch-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-5-preview-Version-2.jpg /2/20/Play Pitch Step 5 Version 2.360p.mp4
  6. 6 Remember, whoever has bid the highest gets to throw down first. Whichever is the first card that they throw becomes the trump suit. Play continues to the left. Everyone throws down one card. The winner of the "hand" is the person who has thrown either the highest card in suit, or the highest trump card. Trump can be played anytime. Trump always beats "in suit." http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/b/b3/Play-Pitch-Step-6-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-6-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/b/b3/Play-Pitch-Step-6-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-6-preview-Version-2.jpg /5/5c/Play Pitch Step 6 Version 2.360p.mp4
  7. 7 The winner of the round gets to take all of the cards that he has won. When everyone is out of cards (which should happen concurrently), the cards are tallied up to see who gets points. http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/7/7b/Play-Pitch-Step-7-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-7-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/7/7b/Play-Pitch-Step-7-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-7-preview-Version-2.jpg /c/c7/Play Pitch Step 7 Version 2.360p.mp4
  8. 8 Points are assigned as follows.
    • 1 point to the lowest trump card played http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/f4/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet1-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet1-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/f4/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet1-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet1-preview-Version-2.jpg /b/ba/Play Pitch Step 8Bullet1 Version 2.360p.mp4
    • 1 point to the highest trump card played http://pad2.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/fb/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet2-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet2-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/f/fb/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet2-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet2-preview-Version-2.jpg /3/31/Play Pitch Step 8Bullet2 Version 2.360p.mp4
    • 1 point to the jack of trump http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/7/7e/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet3-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet3-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad1.whstatic.com/images/thumb/7/7e/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet3-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet3-preview-Version-2.jpg /a/ab/Play Pitch Step 8Bullet3 Version 2.360p.mp4
    • 1 point to "game" (the person with the highest number, if you count the 10 card of any suit as 10 and each jack as 1, queen as 2, king as 3, ace as 4) http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/8/82/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet4-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet4-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/8/82/Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet4-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-8Bullet4-preview-Version-2.jpg /9/9e/Play Pitch Step 8Bullet4 Version 2.360p.mp4
  9. 9 If the highest initial bidder (the person who threw the trump at the beginning of the game) has come up with the points that he bid, he gets those points. If not, he gets negative those points. Meanwhile, anyone else who scores points gets them free of charge (i.e., if the bidder bid 3, and he got high low and game, he gets 3 points. The person who got jack gets 1 point. If the bidder bid 3, and got high and low, he gets negative three points. Whoever got jack gets a point, whoever got game gets a point.) http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/d8/Play-Pitch-Step-9-preview-Version-2.jpg/550px-Play-Pitch-Step-9-preview-Version-2.jpg http://pad3.whstatic.com/images/thumb/d/d8/Play-Pitch-Step-9-preview-Version-2.jpg/300px-Play-Pitch-Step-9-preview-Version-2.jpg /c/cf/Play Pitch Step 9 Version 2.360p.mp4

How to Build a Sideboard

Community Q&A

If you are a serious Magic: the Gathering player, and are looking to play in tournaments, you should consider building a sideboard for your deck. A sideboard is a collection of 15 cards that you do not use in your first game, but will switch out with cards that are mainboard later, before the beginning of games 2 and 3.

Steps

  1. 1 Experiment with cards that compliment your deck, and that help you defeat certain other colors.
  2. 2 After you find certain cards that help out your deck, separate them out from the ones that are too color or type specific.
  3. 3 A sideboard is a collection of emergency cards. Make sure to throw in cards that will help you with multiple problems you may face with your opponent's deck.
  4. 4 Make sure your sideboard consists of 15 cards and no more. It can be less, but there is no reason it should be. More options is always better.
  5. 5 Also when making your sideboard, look at the average mana costs of your mainboard. Make sure the sideboard cards fit in.
  6. 6 When you use your sideboard, do not put in cards from it until your second game, so you know what type of deck your opponent is playing. They may do the same.
  7. 7 When you are actually side boarding cards in and out, make sure that you take out as many cards as you are putting in.
  8. 8 After your second game, if you are going to move on to game three, you may sideboard more cards.
  9. 9 After your games are over, make sure to take out the cards you sideboarded, and put back in the cards that were originally mainboard.
